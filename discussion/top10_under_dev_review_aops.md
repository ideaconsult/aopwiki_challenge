# Top 10 Most Promising AOPs Under Development or Under Review
*Analysis conducted via AOP-Wiki MCP Tool | May 2026*

---

## Methodology & Selection Criteria

AOPs were retrieved across multiple biological domains via keyword searches (oxidative stress, thyroid hormone, radiation, fibrosis, cholestasis, cardiac, coagulation, cataract, etc.). From the full results pool, only AOPs with OECD status **"Under Development"** or **"Under Review"** were retained. Full structural details were fetched for the highest-potential candidates and scored on five criteria:

| Criterion | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **Structural completeness** | Number of KEs and KERs — more links = stronger mechanistic narrative |
| **Assay coverage** | Fraction of KEs with validated measurement methods |
| **Adverse outcome significance** | Human health or ecotoxicological relevance of the AO endpoint |
| **Stressor diversity** | Number of named stressors mapped — real-world applicability |
| **OECD pipeline readiness** | How close the AOP is to meeting OECD endorsement criteria |

### 1. AOP478 — Deposition of Energy Leading to Occurrence of Cataracts
**OECD Status:** Under Review
**MIE:** Deposition of Energy (KE1686)
**Adverse Outcome:** Occurrence of Cataracts (KE2083)
**Key Events:** 8 | **KERs:** 15 | **Stressors:** 1 (ionizing radiation)

**Why it is promising:**
This is the structurally richest AOP in the Under Review pool, with 8 KEs and 15 KERs — an exceptionally high KER density that indicates a deeply interconnected, well-evidenced mechanistic network. The pathway elegantly captures two parallel routes from energy deposition to cataracts: a protein modification branch (energy → oxidative stress → modified/aggregated crystallin proteins → lens opacity) and a genotoxic branch (oxidative DNA damage → DNA strand breaks → inadequate repair → mutations/chromosomal aberrations → cell proliferation → lens opacity). The dual-route convergence on a single adverse outcome strengthens biological plausibility considerably. Its regulatory significance is high — radiation-induced cataracts are now recognised as a deterministic effect at lower doses than previously assumed (per ICRP Publication 118), making this AOP directly actionable for radiological protection dose limits. All molecular and cellular KEs carry validated assays, and the adverse outcome (lens opacification) has well-established clinical grading systems. Under Review status means this AOP is close to OECD endorsement — likely the nearest to the finish line in this cohort.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- Energy Deposition (Monte Carlo, FNTD, TEPC, dosimeters)
- Oxidative Stress (19 methods: H2-DCF-DA, GSH, TBARS, Seahorse)
- Oxidative DNA Damage (comet, ELISA, HPLC-EC, sequencing)
- DNA Strand Breaks (γH2AX, PFGE, TUNEL, comet)
- Inadequate DNA Repair (γH2AX foci, 53BP1, FM-HCR)
- Mutations (Ames, HPRT, TK, PIG-A, transgenic rodent)
- Chromosomal Aberrations (MN, FISH, aCGH, NGS)
- Modified Proteins (mass spectrometry, Western blot, BCA)
- Cell Proliferation (BrdU, Ki-67, CyQuant)
- Cataracts AO (slit-lamp grading, OCT, Scheimpflug imaging)

### 2. AOP470 — Deposition of Energy Leads to Abnormal Vascular Remodeling
**OECD Status:** Under Review
**MIE:** Deposition of Energy (KE1686)
**Adverse Outcome:** Occurrence, Abnormal Vascular Remodeling (KE2069)
**Key Events:** 6 | **KERs:** 12 | **Stressors:** 1 (ionizing radiation)

**Why it is promising:**
Radiation-induced cardiovascular disease (CVD) is increasingly recognised as a major late effect of radiotherapy (thoracic, breast, mediastinal), and growing evidence implicates lower-dose occupational exposures. This AOP formalises the mechanistic chain from energy deposition through a convergent network: oxidative stress → DNA strand breaks feed into altered stress response signalling (NF-κB, MAPK, p38, PKA pathways), which in turn drives both nitric oxide dysregulation and pro-inflammatory mediator release, ultimately causing endothelial dysfunction and abnormal vascular remodeling. With 12 KERs across 6 KEs, the mechanistic web is highly connected. All upstream KEs (oxidative stress, DNA strand breaks, stress signalling, NO levels, pro-inflammatory mediators) have rich assay coverage. The AO itself (abnormal vascular remodeling) has validated histopathological and functional assays (PWV, wire myography, HE staining, Verhoeff-van Gieson). Under Review status and direct alignment with radiation protection policy make this a high priority for completion.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- Energy Deposition (Monte Carlo, FNTD, TEPC, dosimeters)
- Oxidative Stress (19 methods)
- DNA Strand Breaks (comet, γH2AX, PFGE, TUNEL)
- Pro-inflammatory Mediators (RT-qPCR, ELISA, cytokine arrays, IHC)
- Altered Stress Response Signalling (ELISA, Western blot, ChIP, Co-IP, EMSA)
- Nitric Oxide Levels (Griess assay, Western blot, DAF-FM probe, IHC)
- Endothelial Dysfunction (SA-β-gal, BrdU, permeability, ECIS, IHC)
- Abnormal Vascular Remodeling AO (PWV, myography, Scheimpflug, Matrigel)

### 3. AOP144 — Endocytic Lysosomal Uptake Leading to Liver Fibrosis
**OECD Status:** Under Review
**MIE:** Endocytotic Lysosomal Uptake (KE1539)
**Adverse Outcome:** Liver Fibrosis (KE344)
**Key Events:** 7 | **KERs:** 8 | **Stressors:** 8 (multiple nanomaterials and chemicals)

**Why it is promising:**
With 8 named stressors — the highest in the Under Review/Under Development pool — this AOP has exceptional chemical breadth and immediate applicability to nanomaterial risk assessment. The mechanistic chain (lysosomal uptake → lysosomal disruption → mitochondrial dysfunction → cell injury/death → pro-inflammatory mediators → leukocyte recruitment → stellate cell activation → collagen accumulation → liver fibrosis) is mechanistically coherent and biologically well-supported. Liver fibrosis is a critical regulatory endpoint for repeated-dose chemical exposure, directly relevant to OECD TG 407/408 and the EU REACH dossier framework. Under Review status reflects an already mature AOP. Every KE has validated assays — notably, the adverse outcome (liver fibrosis) is measurable by clinical-grade tools including liver biopsy, Ishak fibrosis scoring, and elastography, making the pathway translatable to both preclinical and clinical endpoints. This is among the most translatable AOPs in the entire database.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- Lysosomal Uptake MIE (lysosomal trapping, FRET, confocal, AFM, FACS, ICP-MS)
- Lysosomal Disruption (AO assay, flow cytometry, LysoTracker, HCA)
- Mitochondrial Dysfunction (O2 consumption, ΔΨm, ETS, ATP, PTP, mtDNA, ROS)
- Cell Injury/Death (LDH, MTT, PI staining, TUNEL, caspase)
- Pro-inflammatory Mediators (ELISA, RT-qPCR, cytokine arrays, IHC)
- Leukocyte Recruitment (FC/FACS, IHC, intravital microscopy, Transwell)
- Stellate Cell Activation (gene expression profiling, IGFBP3 assay)
- Collagen Accumulation (Masson Trichrome, Sirius red, hydroxyproline, PCR)
- Liver Fibrosis AO (biopsy, Knodell/Ishak scores, elastography)

### 4. AOP152 — Transthyretin Interference Leading to Adverse Neurodevelopmental Toxicity
**OECD Status:** Under Development
**MIE:** Binding, Transthyretin in serum (KE957)
**Adverse Outcome:** Cognitive function, decreased (KE402)
**Key Events:** 9 | **KERs:** 4 (partially documented) | **Stressors:** 13 (PCBs, flame retardants, pesticides, BPA, etc.)

**Why it is promising:**
This AOP has the largest stressor count in the Under Development pool — 13 named stressors covering PCB congeners, organophosphate flame retardants, polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDEs), BPA, and pesticides — reflecting the broad real-world relevance of TTR-binding as a mechanism of thyroid disruption. The TTR-displacement pathway is mechanistically distinct from the better-known NIS inhibition and TPO inhibition AOPs (already OECD endorsed), representing an important additional route to developmental hypothyroxinemia. The pathway flows from TTR binding → T4 displacement → transient free T4 rise → accelerated T4 clearance → serum and neuronal T4 depletion → hippocampal gene/anatomy/physiology changes → decreased cognitive function, mirroring the mechanistic basis for PCB-associated developmental neurotoxicity observed in human epidemiological studies. The AO (decreased cognitive function, KE402) is measurable by validated behavioural assays (Morris Water Maze, Barnes maze, fear conditioning). The main development gap is formalisation of the KER chain from hippocampal physiology disruption to cognitive AO. With 13 stressors and strong human relevance, completion should be prioritised.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- TTR Binding MIE (radioligand binding, SPR, fluorescence displacement, LC/MS)
- T4 Displacement from TTR (competitive binding, HPLC, qNPA)
- Free T4 (equilibrium dialysis, ultrafiltration, immunoassay, MS/MS)
- T4 Tissue Uptake (same panel as free T4)
- T4 Clearance (UGT/SULT assays, biliary excretion, nuclear receptor assays)
- Serum T4 Decreased (RIA, ELISA, HPLC, LC/MS)
- Neuronal T4 Decreased (RIA, LC-ICP-MS, LC-MS/MS)
- Hippocampal Gene Expression (microarray, RT-PCR, RNA-Seq)
- Hippocampal Anatomy (confocal, TEM, Golgi stain, voxel-based morphometry)
- Hippocampal Physiology (LTP, EPSP/EPSC, synaptic transmission assays)
- Cognitive Function AO (MWM, Barnes, NOR, fear conditioning)

### 5. AOP379 — Binding to ACE2 Leading to Thrombosis and Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation
**OECD Status:** Under Development
**MIE:** Binding to ACE2 (KE1739)
**Adverse Outcome:** Thrombosis and DIC (KE1846)
**Key Events:** 5 | **KERs:** 7 | **Stressors:** 1 (SARS-CoV-2)

**Why it is promising:**
This is the only AOP in the database that explicitly formalises the SARS-CoV-2 pathogenesis mechanism — mapping the molecular cascade from ACE2 binding through viral cell entry, IFN-I antagonism, increased viral replication, diminished Nrf2/ROS protective response, and coagulopathy to thrombosis and DIC. As a disease-mechanism AOP (rather than a chemical toxicology AOP), it represents a novel and important expansion of the AOP framework's scope into infectious disease and pandemic preparedness. The regulatory value is substantial: it supports the development of antiviral drug targets and COVID-19 countermeasures grounded in the AOP framework. All KEs have multiple validated assays — notably the IFN-I antagonism KE has 15 assay methods including replicon systems, co-IP, and mass spectrometry-based proteomics. The AO endpoint (thrombosis/DIC) has clinical standard assays (D-dimer, PT, APTT, platelet count, fibrinogen). The conceptual novelty and public health impact make this a priority for development despite its concise structure.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- ACE2 Binding MIE (SPR, BLI, ELISA)
- SARS-CoV-2 Cell Entry (RT-PCR, RNA-Seq, GFP/luciferase, crystallography)
- IFN-I Antagonism (luciferase reporter, qPCR, Co-IP, Western blot, proteomics)
- SARS-CoV-2 Production (RT-qPCR, RT-LAMP, plaque assays, electron microscopy)
- Diminished ROS Protective Response (Nrf2 reporter, GSH, CAT, SOD, lipid peroxidation)
- Coagulation (platelet count, PT, APTT, D-dimer, fibrinogen, whole blood model)
- Thrombosis and DIC AO (PT, APTT, D-dimer, fibrinogen, platelet count)

### 6. AOP8 — Nuclear Receptor-Induced TH Catabolism Leading to Developmental Hearing Loss
**OECD Status:** Under Development
**MIE:** Activation, Pregnane-X Receptor (KE239)
**Adverse Outcome:** Loss, Cochlear Function (KE319)
**Key Events:** 7 | **KERs:** 8 | **Stressors:** 0 (PCB class implied but not named)

**Why it is promising:**
This AOP describes a mechanistically important and underappreciated route to developmental ototoxicity: nuclear receptor (PXR)-mediated upregulation of thyroid hormone catabolism (glucuronosyltransferases) → accelerated T4 clearance → developmental hypothyroxinemia → hippocampal and cochlear disruption → hearing loss. The pathway is strongly supported by the body of PCB developmental neurotoxicity literature (Goldey, Crofton, Rice, Herr) and is distinct from the direct TPO-inhibition and NIS-inhibition pathways already endorsed by OECD. The ototoxicity adverse outcome (cochlear function loss) is an under-represented AO in AOP-Wiki, making this AOP unique. The MIE is supported by 6 validated HTS assays from ToxCast/Tox21 (ATG_PXRE_CIS, TOX21_PXR_agonist, etc.), and most intermediate KEs carry validated assays. The primary gap is the absence of KER weight-of-evidence narratives and a formal stressor list — both tractable. The AOP is particularly valuable for regulatory assessment of environmental contaminants (PCBs, flame retardants) under developmental neurotoxicity testing frameworks.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- PXR Activation MIE (ToxCast HTS: ATG_PXRE_CIS, TOX21_PXR_agonist, INDIGO Biosciences, NVS_NR_hPXR)
- Glucuronosyltransferase Upregulation (enzyme induction assay, UGT1A1/SULT2A1 expression)
- T4 Clearance (T4 glucuronidation, biliary excretion, HPLC, MS/MS, immunoassay)
- Serum T4 Decreased (RIA, ELISA, HPLC, LC/MS)
- Neuronal T4 Decreased (LC-ICP-MS, HPLC-ICP-MS, LC-MS/MS)
- Hippocampal Gene Expression (microarray, RT-PCR, RNA-Seq)
- Hippocampal Anatomy (confocal, TEM, Golgi stain, VBM)
- Hippocampal Physiology (LTP, EPSP/EPSC, synaptic transmission)

### 7. AOP479 — Mitochondrial Complex Inhibition Leading to Decreased Left Ventricular Function
**OECD Status:** Under Development
**MIE:** Inhibition, Mitochondrial ETC Complexes (KE105)
**Adverse Outcome:** Decrease, Left Ventricular Function (KE2215)
**Key Events:** 5 | **KERs:** 7 | **Stressors:** 0

**Why it is promising:**
Cardiotoxicity is a leading cause of drug attrition in pharmaceutical development and a key concern for industrial chemicals. This AOP links mitochondrial ETC inhibition — a well-documented mechanism for numerous industrial chemicals (rotenone, antimycin A, MPP+) and drugs (anthracyclines, antiretrovirals) — to decreased left ventricular function via the chain: ETC inhibition → mitochondrial dysfunction → oxidative stress → cell injury/death → two converging branches (cardiac remodelling; decreased cardiac contractility) → decreased LV function. The dual downstream branch structure (remodelling and contractility) is mechanistically accurate and reflects the two main clinically distinct routes to heart failure. The MIE and the first three KEs all carry validated assays. The main development gap is assay formalisation for the cardiac remodelling, cardiac contractility, and LV function KEs — a tractable priority. Given the relevance to pharmaceutical safety (drug-induced cardiac failure) and occupational/environmental chemical exposure, this AOP fills an important gap not covered by existing endorsed pathways.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- ETC Complex Inhibition MIE (MTT, Seahorse, luciferin-luciferase ATP assay, cytochrome c binding, enzyme activity)
- Mitochondrial Dysfunction (O2 consumption, ΔΨm, ETS, ATP, PTP, mtDNA damage, ROS)
- Oxidative Stress (19 methods: H2-DCF-DA, GSH, TBARS, Seahorse)
- Cell Injury/Death (LDH, MTT, PI, TUNEL, caspase, Hoechst)
- Cardiac Remodelling (assay methods not yet formalised — development gap)
- Cardiac Contractility Decrease (assay methods not yet formalised — development gap)
- LV Function Decrease AO (assay methods not yet formalised — development gap)

### 8. AOP363 — Thyroperoxidase Inhibition Leading to Altered Visual Function
**OECD Status:** Under Review
**MIE:** Thyroperoxidase Inhibition (KE279)
**Adverse Outcomes:** Increased Mortality (KE351); Decreased Population Growth Rate (KE360)
**Key Events:** 5 | **KERs:** 7 | **Stressors:** 2 (propylthiouracil, methimazole)

**Why it is promising:**
This is a rare AOP that extends thyroid hormone disruption into a completely distinct tissue system — the retina and visual system — via a mechanistically coherent pathway: TPO inhibition → decreased TH synthesis → decreased serum T4 → decreased T3 → altered retinal layer structure → altered visual function → increased mortality/decreased population growth. The adverse outcomes are at the population level, giving this AOP direct value for ecological risk assessment of thyroid-disrupting chemicals (flame retardants, pesticides, industrial chemicals) in aquatic and terrestrial species. The pathway connects to population-level consequences of EDC exposure, a rapidly growing regulatory priority under REACH, the EU Chemicals Strategy for Sustainability, and ECHA/EFSA EDC criteria. Visual function disruption as an AO is unique and under-represented in AOP-Wiki. Two named stressors (PTU, MMI) are the well-validated pharmacological tools for the pathway. All KEs have established assays. Under Review status signals the AOP is approaching OECD submission. KER from visual function loss to mortality/population decline is the primary outstanding gap.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- TPO Inhibition MIE (guaiacol oxidation assay, fluorescent substrate assay, HPLC)
- TH Synthesis Decreased (RIA, ELISA, HPLC/ICP-MS, TIQDT, LC/MS)
- Serum T4 Decreased (RIA, ELISA, HPLC, LC/MS)
- T3 Decreased (RIA, ELISA, HPLC, LC/MS)
- Retinal Layer Structure Altered (EM, light microscopy, antibody staining, HE staining, layer measurement)
- Visual Function Altered (electroretinography, OKR, OMR, light-dark assay, calcium imaging)
- Population AO (population growth rate assay, DEB modelling, ELMs)

### 9. AOP27 — BSEP Inhibition Leading to Cholestatic Liver Injury
**OECD Status:** Under Development
**MIE:** Inhibition, Bile Salt Export Pump ABCB11 (KE41)
**Adverse Outcome:** Cholestasis, Pathology (KE357)
**Key Events:** 6 | **KERs:** 9 | **Stressors:** 0

**Why it is promising:**
Drug-induced liver injury (DILI) via BSEP inhibition is a clinically well-established mechanism responsible for several post-market drug withdrawals (troglitazone, bosentan, cyclosporine). BSEP inhibition is now a standard in vitro safety screen in pharmaceutical development. This AOP formalises the BSEP → cholestasis mechanism with 9 KERs across 6 KEs, making it the most densely connected AOP in the Under Development pool relative to KE count. The mechanistic chain involves two converging routes from bile accumulation: a nuclear receptor transcriptional response route, and a cytokine/inflammation/ROS/peptide oxidation route — both converging on the cholestatic AO. The intermediate KEs (cytokine release, inflammation, ROS) all carry validated assays. The primary gaps are assay formalisation for the MIE (BSEP inhibition) and the AO (cholestasis) — both are tractable, as vesicular transport assays for ABCB11 are established in industry, and clinical cholestasis biomarkers (serum bile acids, bilirubin, ALP, GGT) are standard. High pharmaceutical industry relevance makes formalisation and OECD submission a priority.

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- BSEP Inhibition MIE (vesicular transport assay, Sf9 membrane vesicles — not yet formalised in AOP-Wiki)
- Bile Accumulation (not yet formalised)
- Nuclear Receptor Activation (not yet formalised)
- Cytokine Release (RT-PCR, in situ hybridisation, ELISA, multiplex immunoassay)
- Inflammation (histopathology, ELISA, LC-MS/MS, IHC, DCFDA)
- ROS (H2-DCF-DA, lipid peroxidation, NBT)
- Peptide Oxidation (GSH/GSSG ratio, F2-isoprostanes, H2O2, nitric oxide, lipid peroxidation)
- Cholestasis AO (clinical biochemistry: serum bile acids, bilirubin, ALP, GGT — not yet formalised in AOP-Wiki)

### 10. AOP31 — Hemoglobin Oxidation Leading to Hematotoxicity (Cyanosis)
**OECD Status:** Under Development
**MIE:** Oxidation of heme Fe(II) to Fe(III) (KE213)
**Adverse Outcome:** Cyanosis (KE321)
**Key Events:** 8 | **KERs:** 11 | **Stressors:** 0 (aniline, TNT, 2,4-DNT implied)

**Why it is promising:**
This AOP addresses a toxicologically distinct and well-characterised mechanism — aromatic amine-induced methemoglobinemia — that is relevant to occupational exposure to munitions chemicals (TNT, DNT), industrial aniline compounds, and certain drugs. With 8 KEs and 11 KERs it is one of the more structurally developed Under Development AOPs. The pathway branches from the MIE into three parallel routes (ROS/oxidative stress; RBC haemolysis; haemoglobin adduct formation), all converging on methemoglobinemia, and from there to cyanosis as the AO. The branched structure accurately captures the toxicological complexity of haemotoxic anilines. The oxidative stress KE is richly instrumented (19 assay methods). The primary weakness is that most non-oxidative-stress KEs (alpha-haemoglobin dysregulation, RBC haemolysis, haemoglobin adducts, G6PD downregulation, hepatic RBC congestion, liver/spleen haemosiderosis, methemoglobinemia, cyanosis) lack formalised assays — the most significant development gap in this shortlist. However, all of these endpoints have established clinical and research measurement methods, making formal assay registration the tractable next step. Regulatory value is high for occupational exposure limits and chemical classification (GHS hematotoxicity category).

**Key measurable KEs with assays:**
- Heme Iron Oxidation MIE (met-Hb spectrophotometry, EPR — not yet formalised in AOP-Wiki)
- Oxidative Stress (19 methods: H2-DCF-DA, GSH, TBARS, DHE, Amplex Red, Seahorse)
- RBC Damage/Haemolysis (haematology analyser, haemoglobin spectrophotometry — not yet formalised)
- Haemoglobin Adducts (adductomics, LC-MS/MS — not yet formalised)
- G6PD Downregulation (enzymatic activity assay — not yet formalised)
- Methemoglobinemia (co-oximetry, met-Hb spectrophotometry — not yet formalised)
- Cyanosis AO (clinical observation, pulse oximetry — not yet formalised)

## Cross-Cutting Observations

**Under Review AOPs (AOP478, AOP470, AOP144, AOP363) are closest to OECD endorsement** and should be prioritised for final KER weight-of-evidence narratives and formal stressor registration. All four have structurally sound mechanistic networks and good assay coverage.

**Thyroid hormone disruption is a dominant theme** across ranks 4, 6, and 8 (AOP152, AOP8, AOP363), reflecting an emerging understanding that thyroid system disruption propagates to far more diverse adverse outcomes than the well-known neurodevelopmental endpoints — now including cognitive function, hearing, and visual function. Completing this cluster of AOPs would collectively form a powerful AOP network for thyroid-disrupting chemical assessment.

**Radiation-biology AOPs (AOP478, AOP470)** are especially timely given revised ICRP dose limits for lens and vascular endpoints, growing concern about low-dose occupational/medical radiation, and the needs of the space exploration sector.

**Critical shared development gap:** In five of the top ten (AOP27, AOP31, AOP479, AOP8, AOP379), the MIE or AO KE lacks formally registered assay methods in AOP-Wiki — despite well-established measurement tools existing in the wider literature. Targeted assay registration campaigns for these specific KEs would represent the highest-impact, lowest-effort action to advance this cohort toward OECD endorsement.

## Comparison with Non-OECD Endorsed AOPs (Previous Report)

AOPs in this cohort are generally more advanced structurally than the non-endorsed AOPs identified in the prior report — particularly the four Under Review AOPs — reflecting that OECD review has already driven mechanistic consolidation. However, the non-endorsed pool contained AOPs with higher absolute KE counts (e.g., AOP200 with 20 KEs), indicating that greater breadth does not always accompany formal development status. The strongest candidates from both cohorts share common mechanistic hubs: oxidative stress, DNA damage, and thyroid hormone disruption — pointing to a high-value opportunity for integrated AOP network analysis across the two pools.
