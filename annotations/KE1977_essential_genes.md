## TIER 1: ALPHA SUBUNITS - PORE-FORMING CHANNELS (Absolutely Essential)

### 1. SCN8A (Sodium Voltage-Gated Channel Alpha Subunit 8 / Nav1.6)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:6334 | Ensembl:ENSG00000196876  
**Protein:** Nav1.6 (PR:000014708)  
**Chromosome:** 12q13.13  

**Rationale:**
SCN8A encodes Nav1.6, the MOST abundant voltage-gated sodium channel in the adult CNS. It is:
- Primary sodium channel at axon initial segments (AIS) in mature neurons
- Dominant channel at nodes of Ranvier in myelinated axons
- Replaces Nav1.2 during development
- Critical for action potential initiation and propagation
- Most sensitive to pyrethroid modification
- Essential for repetitive firing

**Key Functions:**
1. **Action potential initiation** at axon initial segment
2. **Action potential propagation** at nodes of Ranvier
3. **Persistent sodium current** generation (higher than other isoforms)
4. **Resurgent current** production (unique feature)
5. **Repetitive neuronal firing** regulation

**Expression Pattern:**
- Expressed in both excitatory AND inhibitory neurons
- High density at axon initial segments
- Concentrated at nodes of Ranvier in myelinated axons
- Replaces Nav1.2 during postnatal development (P7-P14 in mice)

**Evidence - Essential Role:**
**Citation:** Meisler MH, O'Brien JE, Sharkey LM. (2010). Sodium channel gene family: epilepsy mutations, gene interactions and modifier effects. Journal of Physiology 588(Pt 11):1841-1848.  
**DOI:** 10.1113/jphysiol.2010.188482  
**PMID:** 20351042  
**PMC:** PMC2901990  
**Key Finding:** "SCN8A encodes one of the major voltage-gated sodium channels that regulate the initiation and propagation of action potentials in the nervous system."

**Evidence - Pyrethroid Sensitivity:**
**Citation:** Tan J, Soderlund DM. (2012). Differential state-dependent modification of inactivation-deficient Nav1.6 sodium channels by the pyrethroid insecticides S-bioallethrin, tefluthrin and deltamethrin. Neurotoxicology 33(3):384-390.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.neuro.2012.03.007  
**PMID:** 22465659  
**PMC:** PMC3349334  
**Key Finding:** "Deltamethrin modification requires repeated channel activation... These studies provide direct evidence for the preferential binding of deltamethrin and tefluthrin to Nav1.6 channels in the open state."

**Evidence - Localization and Function:**
**Citation:** Hu W, Tian C, Li T, Yang M, Hou H, Shu Y. (2009). Distinct contributions of Nav1.6 and Nav1.2 in action potential initiation and backpropagation. Nature Neuroscience 12(8):996-1002.  
**DOI:** 10.1038/nn.2359  
**PMID:** 19633666  
**Key Finding:** "Nav1.6 is the dominant channel at the AIS and is responsible for rapid AP firing and backpropagation."

**Clinical Relevance:**
- SCN8A mutations cause epileptic encephalopathy
- Gain-of-function → early infantile epilepsy
- Loss-of-function → intellectual disability, autism
- Most common sodium channel gene in developmental disorders

### 2. SCN2A (Sodium Voltage-Gated Channel Alpha Subunit 2 / Nav1.2)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:6326 | Ensembl:ENSG00000136531  
**Protein:** Nav1.2 (PR:000014704)  
**Chromosome:** 2q24.3  

**Rationale:**
SCN2A encodes Nav1.2, the dominant neuronal sodium channel during early development:
- Primary channel in neonatal and early postnatal brain
- Expressed at axon initial segments before Nav1.6 takes over
- Remains in unmyelinated axons throughout life
- Important in GABAergic interneurons
- Sensitive to pyrethroid modification (especially Type II)

**Key Functions:**
1. **Early brain development** - dominant in neonatal period
2. **Action potential initiation** in developing neurons
3. **Persistent in unmyelinated axons** into adulthood
4. **GABAergic interneuron function**
5. **Can compensate for Nav1.6 loss**

**Expression Pattern:**
- HIGH in neonatal brain (first weeks of life)
- Gradually replaced by Nav1.6 at axon initial segments (postnatal weeks 2-4 in rodents)
- Remains high in unmyelinated axons throughout life
- Continues expression in certain interneuron populations

**Evidence - Developmental Role:**
**Citation:** Brunklaus A, Du J, Steckler F, et al. (2020). Biological concepts in human sodium channel epilepsies and their relevance in clinical practice. Epilepsia 61(3):387-399.  
**DOI:** 10.1111/epi.16438  
**PMID:** 32077523  
**PMC:** PMC7155027  
**Key Finding:** "During typical development, SCN2A channels (Nav1.2) are successively replaced by SCN8A channels (Nav1.6) at the axon initial segment and nodes of Ranvier."

**Evidence - Pyrethroid Sensitivity:**
**Citation:** Meacham CA, Brodfuehrer PD, Watkins JA, Shafer TJ. (2008). Developmentally-regulated sodium channel subunits are differentially sensitive to α-cyano containing pyrethroids. Toxicology and Applied Pharmacology 231(2):273-281.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.taap.2008.04.017  
**PMID:** 18538810  
**PMC:** PMC2556394  
**Key Finding:** "Nav1.2 is sensitive to type II pyrethroids but not type I, whereas Nav1.8 is sensitive to both."

**Evidence - Compensation:**
**Citation:** Kearney JA, Plummer NW, Smith MR, et al. (2001). A gain-of-function mutation in the sodium channel gene Scn2a results in seizures and behavioral abnormalities. Neuroscience 102(2):307-317.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0306-4522(00)00479-6  
**PMID:** 11166117

**Clinical Relevance:**
- SCN2A mutations = leading cause of infantile epilepsy and autism
- Gain-of-function (first 3 months) → early infantile epileptic encephalopathy
- Loss-of-function (after 3 months) → intellectual disability, autism without seizures
- Genotype-phenotype correlation strongest in sodium channelopathies

### 3. SCN1A (Sodium Voltage-Gated Channel Alpha Subunit 1 / Nav1.1)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:6323 | Ensembl:ENSG00000144285  
**Protein:** Nav1.1  
**Chromosome:** 2q24.3  

**Rationale:**
SCN1A encodes Nav1.1, preferentially expressed in GABAergic inhibitory interneurons:
- Primary sodium channel in inhibitory interneurons
- Critical for GABAergic neuron excitability
- Loss causes disinhibition → hyperexcitability
- Target of Dravet syndrome mutations
- Sensitive to pyrethroids

**Key Functions:**
1. **Inhibitory interneuron excitability** (primary function)
2. **GABA-mediated inhibition** maintenance
3. **Network balance** (excitation/inhibition)
4. **Seizure threshold** regulation

**Expression Pattern:**
- Highly expressed in GABAergic interneurons (parvalbumin+ cells)
- Lower expression in pyramidal neurons
- Critical for fast-spiking interneurons

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Catterall WA, Kalume F, Oakley JC. (2010). Nav1.1 channels and epilepsy. Journal of Physiology 588(Pt 11):1849-1859.  
**DOI:** 10.1113/jphysiol.2010.187484  
**PMID:** 20194124  
**PMC:** PMC2901991  
**Key Finding:** "Loss-of-function mutations in SCN1A cause Dravet syndrome by reducing the excitability of GABAergic inhibitory neurons."

**Clinical Relevance:**
- SCN1A = most common genetic cause of epilepsy (Dravet syndrome)
- Haploinsufficiency → impaired inhibitory neuron function → seizures
- Temperature-sensitive seizures common

### 4. SCN3A (Sodium Voltage-Gated Channel Alpha Subunit 3 / Nav1.3)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:6328 | Ensembl:ENSG00000138140  
**Protein:** Nav1.3  
**Chromosome:** 2q24.3  

**Rationale:**
SCN3A encodes Nav1.3, primarily expressed during embryonic development:
- Dominant in embryonic and early fetal brain
- Rapidly downregulated postnatally
- Re-expressed after injury
- Involved in neuronal migration
- Can be pyrethroid target during development

**Key Functions:**
1. **Embryonic brain development**
2. **Neuronal migration** during development
3. **Injury response** (re-expression)
4. **Developmental plasticity**

**Expression Pattern:**
- HIGH in embryonic brain
- Decreases dramatically after birth
- Very low in adult CNS (except after injury)
- Re-expressed in some pathological conditions

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Wada A. (2006). Roles of voltage-dependent sodium channels in neuronal development, pain, and neurodegeneration. Journal of Pharmacological Sciences 102(3):253-268.  
**DOI:** 10.1254/jphs.CRJ06012X  
**PMID:** 17072104  
**Key Finding:** "Nav1.3 plays important roles in neuronal development including neurite outgrowth and neuronal migration."

## TIER 2: BETA SUBUNITS - REGULATORY PROTEINS (Highly Important)

### 5. SCN1B (Sodium Voltage-Gated Channel Beta Subunit 1)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:6324  
**Protein:** Navβ1  
**Chromosome:** 19q13.11  

**Rationale:**
SCN1B encodes the β1 regulatory subunit that:
- Modulates sodium channel gating kinetics
- Affects channel trafficking and membrane localization
- Cell adhesion molecule functions
- Influences pyrethroid sensitivity

**Key Functions:**
1. Modulates channel inactivation kinetics
2. Increases channel expression at cell surface
3. Cell-cell adhesion (CAM function)
4. Regulates channel trafficking

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Isom LL. (2002). Beta subunits: players in neuronal hyperexcitability? Novartis Foundation Symposium 241:124-138.  
**DOI:** 10.1002/0470846682.ch9  
**PMID:** 11771644

## TIER 3: CHANNEL INTERACTING PROTEINS (Important Modulators)

### 9. FGF14 (Fibroblast Growth Factor 14)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:2259  

**Rationale:**
FGF14 (Fibroblast Growth Factor Homologous Factor 4) is NOT a growth factor but a voltage-gated sodium channel regulator:
- Directly binds to Nav1.6 C-terminus
- Regulates channel gating and trafficking
- Essential for proper neuronal excitability
- Modulates pyrethroid effects

**Evidence:**

**DOI:** 10.1113/jphysiol.2005.097220

## PYRETHROID SENSITIVITY HIERARCHY

**Most Sensitive:**
1. **SCN8A (Nav1.6)** - Preferential open-state binding
2. **SCN2A (Nav1.2)** - Type II pyrethroid sensitive
3. **SCN1A (Nav1.1)** - Moderate sensitivity

**Sensitivity Factors:**
- State-dependence (open > closed > inactivated)
- Isoform-specific binding sites (PyR1, PyR2)
- Beta subunit composition
- Developmental splice variants (5N vs. 5A)

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Soderlund DM. (2010). State-Dependent Modification of Voltage-Gated Sodium Channels by Pyrethroids. Pesticide Biochemistry and Physiology 97(2):78-86.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.pestbp.2009.06.010  
**PMID:** 20652092  
**PMC:** PMC2905833

## SPLICE VARIANTS (Critical for Understanding Kinetics)

### 5N vs. 5A Exons:
All four brain sodium channel genes (SCN1A, SCN2A, SCN3A, SCN8A) have mutually exclusive exons:
- **Exon 5N** - Neonatal isoform
- **Exon 5A** - Adult isoform

**Functional Differences:**
- 5N isoforms: May limit excitability during development
- 5A isoforms: Predominate in adult brain (>4-fold higher than 5N)
- Pyrethroid sensitivity may differ between isoforms

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Thompson CH, Hawkins NA, Kearney JA, George AL Jr. (2017). CaMKII modulates sodium current in neurons from epileptic Scn2a mutant mice. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 114(7):1696-1701.  
**DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.1615774114  
**PMID:** 28137853  
**PMC:** PMC5320966

**Additional Evidence:**
**Citation:** Gazina EV, Leaw BTW, Richards KL, et al. (2015). "Neonatal" Nav1.2 reduces neuronal excitability and affects seizure susceptibility and behaviour. Human Molecular Genetics 24(5):1457-1468.  
**DOI:** 10.1093/hmg/ddu558  
**PMID:** 25378554

## CLINICAL RELEVANCE TO KE1977

### Why These Genes Matter for Pyrethroid Toxicity:

1. **Age-Dependent Vulnerability:**
   - Neonates: High SCN2A → High Nav1.2 expression → Type II pyrethroid sensitive
   - Adults: High SCN8A → High Nav1.6 expression → Most pyrethroid sensitive

2. **Cell-Type Specific Effects:**
   - GABAergic neurons (SCN1A) affected → Disinhibition → Hyperexcitability
   - Pyramidal neurons (SCN8A/SCN2A) affected → Direct excitability changes

3. **Mutation Phenocopying:**
   - Pyrethroid-induced channel dysfunction may phenocopy genetic mutations
   - Gain-of-function mutations → Early seizures (like pyrethroid effects)
