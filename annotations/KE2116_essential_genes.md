## TIER 1: MASTER REGULATORY TRANSCRIPTION FACTORS (Absolutely Essential)

### 1. OLIG2 (Oligodendrocyte Transcription Factor 2)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:10215 | Ensembl:ENSG00000205927  
**Protein:** PR:000013938  
**Chromosome:** 21q22.11  

**Rationale:**
OLIG2 is THE master regulator of oligodendrocyte specification and differentiation. It is:
- Required for oligodendrocyte lineage specification from neural progenitors
- Essential for OPC differentiation into mature oligodendrocytes
- Functions as both transcriptional activator and repressor depending on developmental stage
- Olig2 knockout mice completely lack oligodendrocytes
- Continuously expressed throughout OPC → mature oligodendrocyte progression

**Key Functions:**
1. Specifies oligodendrocyte lineage identity
2. Promotes OPC proliferation
3. Triggers differentiation program onset
4. Recruits chromatin modifiers (SETDB1) for gene repression
5. Directly activates myelin genes in cooperation with other factors

**Evidence - Essentiality:**
**Citation:** Lu QR, Sun T, Zhu Z, et al. (2002). Common developmental requirement for Olig function indicates a motor neuron/oligodendrocyte connection. Cell 109(1):75-86.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0092-8674(02)00678-5  
**PMID:** 11955448  
**Key Finding:** "In Olig1/2 double-mutant mice, oligodendrocyte differentiation was abolished."

**Evidence - Transcriptional Repressor Function:**
**Citation:** Zhang S, Zhu X, Gui X, et al. (2022). OLIG2 regulates transcriptional repression during myelinogenesis in rodents. Nature Communications 13:1423.  
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-022-29068-z  
**PMID:** 35301320  
**Key Finding:** "OLIG2 functions as a transcriptional repressor by recruiting SETDB1 to build H3K9me3 modification on genomic loci destined to be downregulated for OPC differentiation."

**Evidence - Clinical Relevance:**
**Citation:** Takebayashi H, Nabeshima Y, et al. (2000). Dynamic expression of basic helix-loop-helix Olig family members. Mechanisms of Development 99(1-2):143-148.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0925-4773(00)00466-4  
**PMID:** 11091082

### 2. SOX10 (SRY-Box Transcription Factor 10)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:6663 | Ensembl:ENSG00000100146  
**Protein:** PR:000015441  
**Chromosome:** 22q13.1  

**Rationale:**
SOX10 is the second master regulator, essential for terminal oligodendrocyte differentiation and myelin gene expression. It is:
- Required for terminal differentiation (not OPC specification)
- Directly activates myelin genes (MBP, PLP, MAG)
- Works synergistically with OLIG1 and MYRF
- Sox10-deficient mice have normal OPC numbers but fail to express myelin genes
- Essential for oligodendrocyte survival after axon wrapping

**Key Functions:**
1. Drives terminal differentiation program
2. Direct activation of myelin structural genes
3. Cooperates with OLIG1 to activate MBP transcription
4. Induces MYRF expression
5. Required for oligodendrocyte survival post-differentiation

**Evidence - Essentiality:**
**Citation:** Stolt CC, Rehberg S, Ader M, et al. (2002). Terminal differentiation of myelin-forming oligodendrocytes depends on the transcription factor Sox10. Genes & Development 16(2):165-170.  
**DOI:** 10.1101/gad.215802  
**PMID:** 11799060  
**Key Finding:** "In Sox10-deficient mice, there are no PLP-, MAG-, or MBP-positive cells... even on sections of newborn homozygous mice, we detected <5% of the MBP- or PLP-positive cells found in wild-type littermates."

**Evidence - Direct Myelin Gene Activation:**
**Citation:** Li H, Lu Y, Smith HK, Richardson WD. (2007). Olig1 and Sox10 interact synergistically to drive myelin basic protein transcription in oligodendrocytes. Journal of Neuroscience 27(52):14375-14382.  
**DOI:** 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.4456-07.2007  
**PMID:** 18160645  
**Key Finding:** "Olig1 associates physically with Sox10, and the Olig1/Sox10 complex activates mbp (myelin basic protein) transcription via conserved DNA sequence motifs."

**Evidence - Cooperation with MYRF:**
**Citation:** Hornig J, Fröb F, Vogl MR, et al. (2013). The transcription factors Sox10 and Myrf define an essential regulatory network module in differentiating oligodendrocytes. PLOS Genetics 9(10):e1003907.  
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pgen.1003907  
**PMID:** 24204311  
**Key Finding:** "Once induced, Myrf physically interacts and functionally cooperates with its inducer Sox10 to activate myelin genes."

## TIER 2: CRITICAL DIFFERENTIATION REGULATORS (Highly Important)

### 3. MYRF (Myelin Regulatory Factor)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:745 | Ensembl:ENSG00000124920  
**Protein:** Myelin regulatory factor  
**Chromosome:** 11q12.2  

**Rationale:**
MYRF is a membrane-associated transcription factor essential for driving the myelination program. It is:
- Directly induced by SOX10
- Auto-proteolytically cleaves to activate myelin genes
- Forms trimers to bind DNA
- Essential for terminal differentiation and myelination

**Key Functions:**
1. Activated by SOX10 during differentiation
2. Directly activates myelin structural genes
3. Works synergistically with SOX10
4. Essential for myelination program execution

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Hornig J, et al. (2013). PLOS Genetics 9(10):e1003907.  
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pgen.1003907  
**PMID:** 24204311

**Additional Evidence:**
**Citation:** Bujalka H, Koenning M, Jackson S, et al. (2013). MYRF is a membrane-associated transcription factor that autoproteolytically cleaves to directly activate myelin genes. PLOS Biology 11(8):e1001625.  
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pbio.1001625  
**PMID:** 23966833

### 4. OLIG1 (Oligodendrocyte Transcription Factor 1)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:116448 | Ensembl:ENSG00000184221  
**Protein:** Oligodendrocyte transcription factor 1  
**Chromosome:** 21q22.11  

**Rationale:**
OLIG1 is a paralog of OLIG2 with distinct but complementary functions:
- Not required for OPC specification (unlike OLIG2)
- Essential for OPC differentiation into myelinating oligodendrocytes
- Works synergistically with SOX10 to activate myelin genes
- Required for remyelination

**Key Functions:**
1. Essential for OPC → mature oligodendrocyte transition
2. Cooperates with SOX10 to activate MBP
3. Critical for myelin gene expression (MBP, PLP, MAG)
4. Important for remyelination in disease

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Li H, et al. (2007). Journal of Neuroscience 27(52):14375-14382.  
**DOI:** 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.4456-07.2007  
**PMID:** 18160645

**Additional Evidence:**
**Citation:** Xin M, Yue T, Ma Z, et al. (2005). Myelinogenesis and axonal recognition by oligodendrocytes in brain are uncoupled in Olig1-null mice. Journal of Neuroscience 25(6):1354-1365.  
**DOI:** 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.3034-04.2005  
**PMID:** 15703389

### 5. NKX2.2 (NK2 Homeobox 2)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:4821 | Ensembl:ENSG00000171189  
**Protein:** Homeobox protein Nkx-2.2  
**Chromosome:** 20p11.22  

**Rationale:**
NKX2.2 is a homeodomain transcription factor that:
- Collaborates with OLIG2 to promote oligodendrocyte fate
- Essential for OPC differentiation
- Expression overlaps with OLIG2 just before OPC formation
- Required for proper oligodendrocyte generation

**Key Functions:**
1. Cooperates with OLIG2 for OPC specification
2. Suppresses alternative neuronal fates
3. Promotes oligodendrocyte differentiation when combined with OLIG2
4. Directly regulates myelin gene expression

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Zhou Q, Choi G, Anderson DJ. (2001). The bHLH transcription factor Olig2 promotes oligodendrocyte differentiation in collaboration with Nkx2.2. Neuron 31(5):791-807.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0896-6273(01)00414-7  
**PMID:** 11567617  
**Key Finding:** "Coexpression of Olig2 with Nkx2.2 in the spinal cord promotes ectopic and precocious oligodendrocyte differentiation."

## TIER 4: NEGATIVE REGULATORS (Inhibitors of Differentiation)

### 9. ID2 and ID4 (Inhibitor of DNA Binding 2 and 4)

**Gene IDs:** ID2: NCBI Gene:3398 | ID4: NCBI Gene:3400  

**Rationale:**
ID proteins inhibit differentiation by:
- Sequestering bHLH transcription factors like OLIG2
- Maintaining OPC proliferative state
- Must be downregulated for differentiation to proceed

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Kondo T, Raff M. (2000). Oligodendrocyte precursor cells reprogrammed to become multipotential CNS stem cells. Science 289(5485):1754-1757.  
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.289.5485.1754  
**PMID:** 10976069

---

### 10. HES5 (Hes Family BHLH Transcription Factor 5)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:388585  

**Rationale:**
HES5 is a Notch signaling effector that:
- Inhibits oligodendrocyte differentiation
- Maintains progenitor state
- Must be downregulated for differentiation

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Liu A, Li J, Marin-Husstege M, et al. (2006). A molecular insight of Hes5-dependent inhibition of myelin gene expression. Molecular and Cellular Biology 26(4):1256-1266.  
**DOI:** 10.1128/MCB.26.4.1256-1266.2006
