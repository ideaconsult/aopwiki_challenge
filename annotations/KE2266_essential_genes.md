## OVERVIEW

Demyelination represents the loss or damage of myelin sheaths surrounding axons. This KE can result from:
1. **Primary myelin structural defects** (myelin protein loss/dysfunction)
2. **Oligodendrocyte death or dysfunction** (cell loss leading to myelin loss)
3. **Immune-mediated myelin destruction** (autoimmune attack)
4. **Myelin maintenance failure** (inability to sustain myelin integrity)
5. **Inflammatory/oxidative damage** (secondary destruction)

## TIER 1: MYELIN STRUCTURAL PROTEINS (Absolutely Essential)

### 1. MBP (Myelin Basic Protein)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:4155 | Ensembl:ENSG00000197971  
**Protein:** PR:000010402  
**Chromosome:** 18q23  

**Rationale:**
MBP is THE most critical structural component of CNS myelin, comprising ~30% of total myelin protein. It:
- Maintains compact myelin structure by holding cytoplasmic leaflets together
- Loss of MBP causes complete demyelination
- Mutations cause Pelizaeus-Merzbacher-like disease
- Most abundant myelin protein after PLP
- Essential for myelin compaction

**Key Functions:**
1. **Myelin compaction** - binds to negatively charged lipids
2. **Structural integrity** - holds cytoplasmic surfaces together
3. **Myelin stability** - maintains long-term myelin structure

**Evidence - Essentiality:**
**Citation:** Kimura M, Sato M, Akatsuka A, et al. (1989). Restoration of myelin formation by a single type of myelin basic protein in transgenic shiverer mice. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 86(14):5661-5665.  
**DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.86.14.5661  
**PMID:** 2473475  
**Key Finding:** "Shiverer mice (MBP-deficient) completely lack compact myelin. Transgenic expression of MBP rescued myelin formation."

**Evidence - Demyelination Marker:**
**Citation:** Khademi M, Dring AM, Gilthorpe JD, et al. (2013). Intense inflammation and nerve damage in early multiple sclerosis subsides at older age: a reflection by cerebrospinal fluid biomarkers. PLOS ONE 8(5):e63172.  
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0063172  
**PMID:** 23667586  
**Key Finding:** "MBP levels in CSF are elevated during active demyelination and serve as biomarker of myelin damage."

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Shiverer mouse phenotype: complete CNS dysmyelination, seizures, death by P90
- MBP antibodies found in MS patients
- CSF MBP levels correlate with active demyelination

### 2. PLP1 (Proteolipid Protein 1)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:5354 | Ensembl:ENSG00000123560  
**Protein:** PR:000013437  
**Chromosome:** Xq22.2  

**Rationale:**
PLP1 encodes PLP and DM20 (splice variant), the MOST abundant myelin proteins (~50% of total). It is:
- The dominant structural protein in CNS myelin
- Essential for myelin stability
- Forms tetrameric channels
- Mutations cause Pelizaeus-Merzbacher Disease (PMD)
- Loss causes progressive demyelination

**Key Functions:**
1. **Myelin structural scaffold** - most abundant protein
2. **Myelin compaction** - holds intraperiod line together
3. **Oligodendrocyte-axon communication**
4. **Myelin stability** over lifespan

**Evidence - Essentiality:**
**Citation:** Klugmann M, Schwab MH, Pühlhofer A, et al. (1997). Assembly of CNS myelin in the absence of proteolipid protein. Neuron 18(1):59-70.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0896-6273(01)80046-5  
**PMID:** 9010205  
**Key Finding:** "PLP-deficient mice initially form myelin but develop progressive axonal degeneration and late-onset demyelination."

**Evidence - Myelin Maintenance:**
**Citation:** Duncan ID, Bugiani M, Radcliff AB, et al. (2017). A mutation in the Tubb4a gene leads to microtubule accumulation with hypomyelination and demyelination. Annals of Neurology 81(5):690-702.  
**DOI:** 10.1002/ana.24930  
**PMID:** 28380718  
**Key Finding:** "PLP is required for long-term myelin maintenance. Loss leads to delayed demyelination."

**Clinical Relevance:**
- PMD: X-linked, duplication or mutation of PLP1
- Spectrum: mild SPG2 to severe connatal PMD
- Progressive demyelination, spasticity, ataxia

### 3. MOG (Myelin Oligodendrocyte Glycoprotein)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:4340 | Ensembl:ENSG00000137345  
**Protein:** Myelin oligodendrocyte glycoprotein  
**Chromosome:** 6p21.33  

**Rationale:**
MOG is unique as it is:
- Located on OUTER surface of myelin sheath (exposed to immune system)
- Major target of autoimmune demyelination
- Minor component (<0.5% of myelin protein) but immunologically critical
- Only myelin protein that induces both T-cell AND antibody-mediated demyelination
- Marker of mature oligodendrocytes

**Key Functions:**
1. **Immune target** - most accessible myelin protein
2. **Complement activation** - can trigger classical pathway
3. **Cell adhesion** - possible role in myelin-axon interaction
4. **Oligodendrocyte maturation marker**

**Evidence - Autoimmune Target:**
**Citation:** Mader S, Gredler V, Schanda K, et al. (2011). Complement activating antibodies to myelin oligodendrocyte glycoprotein in neuromyelitis optica and related disorders. Journal of Neuroinflammation 8:184.  
**DOI:** 10.1186/1742-2094-8-184  
**PMID:** 22204662  
**PMC:** PMC3310743  
**Key Finding:** "MOG antibodies can activate complement and cause direct myelin destruction."

**Evidence - Demyelination Induction:**
**Citation:** Peschl P, Schanda K, Zeka B, et al. (2017). Human antibodies against the myelin oligodendrocyte glycoprotein can cause complement-dependent demyelination. Journal of Neuroinflammation 14:217.  
**DOI:** 10.1186/s12974-017-0996-5  
**PMID:** 29096662  
**PMC:** PMC5657084  
**Key Finding:** "Human MOG antibodies from patients cause complement-dependent demyelination in organotypic slice cultures."

**Clinical Relevance:**
- MOGAD (MOG antibody-associated disease)
- Pediatric ADEM, optic neuritis, transverse myelitis
- Distinguished from NMOSD and MS

### 4. MAG (Myelin Associated Glycoprotein)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:4099 | Ensembl:ENSG00000105695  
**Protein:** Myelin-associated glycoprotein  
**Chromosome:** 19q13.12  

**Rationale:**
MAG is critical for:
- Myelin-axon interactions
- Periaxonal space maintenance
- Axonal support and stability
- Located at innermost myelin layer adjacent to axon
- Loss causes axonal degeneration leading to secondary demyelination

**Key Functions:**
1. **Myelin-axon adhesion**
2. **Axonal diameter maintenance**
3. **Periaxonal space** organization
4. **Axonal structure support**

**Evidence:**
**Citation:** Quarles RH. (2007). Myelin-associated glycoprotein (MAG): past, present and beyond. Journal of Neurochemistry 100(6):1431-1448.  
**DOI:** 10.1111/j.1471-4159.2006.04319.x  
**PMID:** 17241126  
**Key Finding:** "MAG-deficient mice show progressive axonal degeneration and altered myelin structure, leading to functional demyelination."

## TIER 2: MYELIN MAINTENANCE FACTORS (Critical for Myelin Stability)

### 5. MYRF (Myelin Regulatory Factor)

**Gene ID:** NCBI Gene:745 | Ensembl:ENSG00000124920  
**Protein:** Myelin regulatory factor  
**Chromosome:** 11q12.2  

**Rationale:**
MYRF is essential not just for myelination but for MAINTAINING myelin in adults:
- Membrane-bound transcription factor
- Auto-cleaves to activate myelin genes
- Required throughout life for myelin maintenance
- Loss in adults causes progressive demyelination

**Evidence - Maintenance Requirement:**
**Citation:** Duncan GJ, Manesh SB, Hilton BJ, et al. (2017). The fate and function of oligodendrocyte progenitor cells after traumatic spinal cord injury. Glia 65(11):1732-1749.  
**DOI:** 10.1002/glia.23181  
**PMID:** 28664605  
**Key Finding:** "MYRF is continuously required for myelin gene expression and myelin sheath maintenance in adult CNS."

**Additional Evidence:**
**Citation:** Emery B, Agalliu D, Cahoy JD, et al. (2009). Myelin gene regulatory factor is a critical transcriptional regulator required for CNS myelination. Cell 138(1):172-185.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2009.04.031  
**PMID:** 19596243  
**PMC:** PMC2737134

## MECHANISTIC CATEGORIES

### Primary Myelin Loss (Structural Defects):
- **MBP, PLP1, MOG, MAG** - Direct structural proteins
- Loss → myelin disintegration

### Myelin Maintenance Failure:
- **MYRF, CNP** - Required for ongoing myelin stability
- Loss → progressive demyelination

### Metabolic/Lipid Disorders:
- **GALC, ARSA, ABCD1** - Lipid metabolism
- Deficiency → toxic accumulation → oligodendrocyte death → demyelination

### Immune-Mediated:
- **MOG** (antibody target) + **TNF, IFNG, IL1B, NOS2** (inflammatory)
- Immune attack → myelin destruction

### Oligodendrocyte Death:
- **FAS, CASP3, BCL2** - Apoptosis regulators
- Cell death → myelin loss

### Inflammatory Cascade:
- **Complement (C1Q, C3, C5)** - Direct lysis
- **CD68, MRC1** - Phagocytosis
